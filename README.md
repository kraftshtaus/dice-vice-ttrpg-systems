## About the Game

Have you ever wondered whether humanity is truly alone in the universe?

Now imagine a civilization capable of reaching millions of stars within seconds and transforming even the most hostile planets into artificial paradises.

It sounds wonderful.

Then something happened.

The universe began to fade.

Stars disappeared, once-stable regions of space became uninhabitable, and the laws that held civilization together started to collapse. Scientists rushed to preserve whatever could still be saved, while corporations, rulers, and opportunists attempted to profit from the coming catastrophe.

As the universe continued to die, the old Empires fled toward one of its last surviving regions.

They came to protect their legacy, their honour, and the lives of those who depended on them. Some arrived as saviours. Others came as conquerors. Many simply wanted to survive.

But the Empires brought their old conflicts with them.

Revolutionary movements, cults, criminal organisations, political conspiracies, and independent factions began fighting for control over what remained.

Chaos created more chaos.

No one knows what will survive, who can still be trusted, or whether the edge of the universe represents humanity’s final refuge—or its final grave.

Welcome to **Hollow Abyss**.

## Game Overview

**Hollow Abyss** is an original tabletop role-playing game set in a dying science-fiction universe.

Players take the roles of mercenaries, outcasts, explorers, criminals, diplomats, engineers, and survivors attempting to build a life among collapsing Empires and hostile worlds.

The game focuses on:

- survival in unstable environments;
- tactical conflicts and resource management;
- political relationships between factions;
- equipment and character specialisation;
- moral compromise;
- personal degradation and the struggle to remain human;
- consequences that continue beyond a single encounter.

The characters are not destined heroes.

They are people and creatures attempting to survive in a universe that no longer guarantees a future.

## Core Design Principles

### Survival Has a Price

Resources, equipment, allies, health, and reputation are limited.

Characters must decide what they are willing to sacrifice to survive and what part of themselves they refuse to lose.

### Equipment Matters

Weapons and armour are not simple numerical bonuses.

Different weapon categories create different tactical effects, while armour protects separate parts of the body and can influence how characters approach combat.

### Characters Are Defined by Their Choices

Character development is not limited to increasing basic statistics.

Players gain new abilities, improve equipment, develop social connections, interact with factions, and choose how far they are willing to go for power.

### The World Reacts

The consequences of a decision may affect:

- faction relationships;
- access to legal or illegal markets;
- future missions;
- settlements and colonies;
- individual non-player characters;
- the character’s reputation;
- the political balance of an entire region.

## Core Resolution System

Hollow Abyss uses pools of twelve-sided dice.

When a character attempts a difficult action, the player creates a dice pool based on the relevant attribute, skill, equipment, and circumstances.

Each die is interpreted separately:

| Roll | Result |
|---:|---|
| 1–2 | Failure |
| 3–7 | Neutral result |
| 8–11 | Success |
| 12 | Critical success |

The difficulty of an action determines how many successes are required.

Critical successes may provide additional benefits depending on the action, ability, weapon, or situation.

The system is designed to make every individual die meaningful while keeping the final result easy to read.

## Core Attributes

Characters are built around four primary attributes:

### Limit

Represents endurance, resilience, physical pressure, willpower, and the ability to continue under extreme conditions.

### Empathy

Represents emotional awareness, communication, social intuition, persuasion, and understanding other beings.

### Coordination

Represents movement, reaction speed, accuracy, balance, and control over the character’s body.

### Mastery

Represents technical knowledge, professional training, craftsmanship, analysis, and the ability to use complex equipment.

Different actions may combine an attribute with a relevant skill, ability, item, or situational modifier.

---

## Combat

Combat in Hollow Abyss is designed to be dangerous without becoming unnecessarily long.

Most encounters are intended to last approximately three to ten rounds, depending on the number of participants, enemy strength, available equipment, and the tactical decisions made by the players.

Combat focuses on:

- positioning;
- weapon properties;
- armour protection;
- limited resources;
- conditions and vulnerabilities;
- cooperation between characters;
- choosing when to retreat.

Victory does not always mean destroying every enemy.

Escaping, completing an objective, protecting an ally, stealing an item, or surviving until extraction may be more important than eliminating the opposition.

---

## Weapon Categories

Weapons may possess special tactical properties.

### Elemental Weapons

Elemental attacks can apply **Vulnerability**, allowing different damage sources to interact and create stronger combined effects.

### Crushing Weapons

Crushing attacks can apply **Stun**, reducing the target’s effectiveness and disrupting its actions.

### Piercing Weapons

Piercing attacks can temporarily reduce or bypass armour protection.

### Laser Weapons

Laser weapons provide high offensive potential but may overheat when used carelessly.

Severe overheating can damage the weapon, injure its user, or cause an explosion.

Weapon effects are intended to encourage cooperation and tactical combinations rather than relying only on raw damage.

---

## Armour System

Armour protects individual parts of the body:

- head;
- arms;
- torso;
- legs.

This allows characters to combine different pieces of equipment and build protection according to their role, resources, and preferred tactics.

Armour categories include:

- combat armour;
- powered armour;
- adaptive polymorphic armour;
- specialised experimental equipment.

Damage to an unprotected or poorly protected body part may create additional consequences beyond the loss of health.

---

## Character Development

The maximum character level is currently planned to be **10**.

Character advancement provides access to new abilities and improvements rather than only increasing basic numbers.

Abilities are acquired through the **Soul Shop**.

### Standard Abilities

- Cost: 10 experience points
- Recommended limit: 3 abilities

### Perfect Abilities

- Cost: 25 experience points
- Recommended limit: 2 abilities

A character may normally possess no more than five major abilities.

To acquire an additional ability beyond this limit, the character must permanently abandon an existing one. Spent experience is not refunded.

This creates meaningful choices and prevents characters from eventually becoming capable of performing every role.

---

## Classes and Archetypes

Hollow Abyss avoids relying only on traditional heroic fantasy classes.

Its character concepts are built around professions, social roles, survival strategies, personal weaknesses, and the way individuals adapted to life at the edge of existence.

Some character paths may include:

- diplomats;
- engineers;
- mercenaries;
- independent scavengers;
- Imperial operatives;
- rejected members of society;
- addicts;
- gamblers;
- homeless survivors;
- cannibals;
- other morally compromised or socially excluded individuals.

A character’s class is not necessarily an identity they are proud of.

It represents the tools, habits, experience, and damage that helped them survive.

---

## Factions

The setting is shaped by three broad political positions.

### The Rejected

Those abandoned, persecuted, exploited, or considered expendable by the dominant powers.

They often survive through underground communities, criminal networks, improvised technology, and mutual dependence.

### The Independents

Individuals and organisations that refuse direct Imperial control.

They may include merchants, scavengers, mercenaries, explorers, private settlements, and neutral organisations.

### The Imperials

Representatives, citizens, soldiers, and institutions of the old Empires.

They possess authority, infrastructure, resources, and advanced technology, but are divided by rivalry, tradition, and political ambition.

Faction membership affects access to equipment, information, missions, settlements, and potential allies.

---

## Equipment Improvement

Characters may obtain and improve equipment through three major approaches.

### Legal Acquisition

Reliable and officially supported equipment obtained through authorised traders, organisations, or Imperial institutions.

Legal equipment is safer but may be expensive, regulated, or unavailable to characters with a poor reputation.

### Black Market

Illegal equipment, prohibited technology, stolen components, and experimental modifications.

Black-market items may provide unusual advantages but often carry risks, hidden defects, or political consequences.

### Independent Modification

Characters may create, repair, and improve equipment themselves.

This method can reduce costs and provide unique results, but requires knowledge, tools, components, and time.

---

## Rest and Recovery

A full rest normally requires at least six hours in reasonably safe conditions.

Rest may restore health, resources, abilities, or other character functions depending on the current rules and conditions.

Sleeping in a dangerous environment does not necessarily provide the same benefits as resting in a protected settlement, station, ship, or medical facility.

## Current Development Status

**Hollow Abyss is currently in active development.**

The project already includes or is developing:

- the core d12 dice-pool system;
- four core attributes;
- character creation;
- classes and subclasses;
- combat rules;
- weapon properties;
- body-part armour;
- character abilities;
- equipment progression;
- factions and reputation;
- legal and illegal markets;
- science-fiction worldbuilding;
- Game Master materials;
- balance and playtesting revisions.

The rules may change as the system continues to be tested and expanded.

---

## Project Goals

Hollow Abyss is being designed to provide:

- tactical but readable combat;
- meaningful equipment choices;
- characters with clear mechanical identities;
- long-term consequences;
- a setting where politics and survival are connected;
- support for groups of different sizes;
- enough depth for campaigns without overwhelming players with unnecessary rules.

The system is intended for players who enjoy dark science fiction, morally difficult decisions, faction conflicts, dangerous exploration, and mechanically distinct characters.
